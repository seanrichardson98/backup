\begin{proof}
	Let $X$, $Y$, and $Z$ denote compact Hausdorff spaces and take $f: X \to Y$ and $g: Y \to Z$ be continuous functions between them.
	
	Further denote the semirings as defined in claim \toadd{ref} by $J(X)$, $J(Y)$, and $J(Z)$. Additionally, let $i_X: J(X) \to K(X)$, $i_Y: J(Y) \to K(Y)$, and $i_Z: J(Z) \to K(Z)$ denote the ring completions of each semiring as in definition \toadd{ref}. Further, let $J(g): J(Z) \to J(Y)$ and $J(f): J(Y) \to J(X)$ denote the homomorphism of semirings as described in claim \toadd{ref}.  Finally, let the functions $K(g): K(Z) \to K(Y)$, $K(f): K(Y) \to K(X)$, and $K(f \circ  g): K(Z) \to K(X)$ be the unique functions such that the following composition identities hold.
	\begin{align*}
		K(f) \circ i_X &= i_Y \circ J(f)\\
		K(g) \circ i_Y &= i_Z \circ J(g)\\
		K(f \circ g) \circ i_X &= i_Z \circ J(f \circ g)
	\end{align*}	
Additionally note that $J(f \circ g) = J(g) \circ J(f)$ follows from the pullback property of \toadd{ref} $(f\circ g)^{*}(E) = g^{*}(f^{*}(E))$ on a bundle $E$ by the following computation on an element $[E] \in J(X)$.
	\begin{align*}
		J(f \circ g)([E]) 
		= [(f \circ g)^{*}(E)]
		= [g^{*}(f^{*}(E))] 
		= J(g)([f^{*}(E)])
		= J(g)(J(f)([E]))
	\end{align*}
	
Substitutions of the preceding result together with the earlier composition identities allows for the following result.
	\begin{align*}
		(K(g) \circ 	K(f)) \circ i_X 
		= K(g) \circ (i_Y \circ J(f)) 
		= (i_Z \circ J(Y)) \circ J(f)
		= i_Z \circ J(f \circ g)
	\end{align*}
And so $K(f) \circ K(g)$ fulfills the defining property of $K(f \circ g)$. Because the function $K(f \circ g)$ is the unique function fulfilling this property, it must be that $K(f) \circ K(g) = K(f \circ g)$. \end{proof}
